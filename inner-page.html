The Robotics Programme

The Robotics Programme is an end-to-end programme touching on the various major disciplines that come together to make up robotics. Each module of the programme represents an entire eld on their own Each session will round of with a mini project where students get to put together everything they have learnt, and each mini project is meant to foster collaboration between students. Finally, there will be an overall project to test everything learnt from each module.

Outcomes

These are the following outcomes expected after students have enrolled in the programme:

  1. To figure out whether they truly want to pursue Engineering as a career path.
  2. To be able to build and programme their own robot from scratch.
  3. With skills from various disciplines, students should be empowered to believe they can do anything.
  4. Students should be able to do amazing things outside of just robotics. With this knowledge they should expect to be able to develop their own products which they can sell to the mass market, solve problems within their schools and communities, as well as gain meaningful employment in an emerging industry even before admission into university.
  5. This programme is meant to help enrich studentsâ€™ university experience.

Modules Under the Programme

Mechanical Design and Fabrication

This will teach students how to make physical products right from conceptualization to realization. Skills under this module include: Sketching, Computer Aided Design (CAD), Kinematics, Dynamics and Mechanics of Materials and Measurement and Fabrication.

Electrical/Electronic Design and Fabrication

This will teach students how to make electronic products right from conceptualization to realization. Skills under this module include: Design and Simulation, Soldering and Printed Circuit Board (PCB) Design and Fabrication.

Computer Programming and Algorithms

Algorithms and computer programming are usually taught as one thing, but in this programme they are going to be taught as two separate things. In this module, students will learn the following skills: Algorithm Analysis, Numerical Methods, Control Systems Design and Programming Languages.

Robotics

This is the final module, that combines all the skills learnt in the previous modules. In this module students will learn about the following: Sensing and Perception, Localization and Odometry and Navigation and Obstacle Avoidance.
